First Generation (1940s-1950s): The Dawn of Computing

Vacuum Tubes Era

Classic ENIAC View

The first generation relied on vacuum tubes, birthing electronic computing. Machines like ENIAC (1945) weighed 30 tons, used 18,000 tubes, and calculated WWII ballistics for the U.S. military.

  • Innovations: Binary code and von Neumann architecture.
  • Impacts: Scientific calculations revolutionized.
  • Challenges: High heat and frequent failures.
